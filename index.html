Overview

What is CARIA?

CARIA (Campaign Approvals Rotation Instructions Attribution) is the platform where agencies and sales houses create Copy Rotation Instructions — telling traffic departments exactly which creatives should air in which spots.

Process

The 3-step workflow

Every CRI follows the same three steps — from metadata to detail blocks to sending.

01

Edit Copy

Add clock numbers, set campaign dates, fill in client/product details, and define mandatory fields like copy status and delivery info.

02

Copy Relationships

Create detail blocks that tell traffic exactly which creative runs on which stations, dates, and time bands. This is the most complex step.

03

Review & Send

Preview the CRI, check for clashes, tick the traffic department checkboxes, and send. Always send to both Channel 4 Traffic and C4 Streaming.

Getting Started

Creating a CRI

There are two ways to create a CRI — from a media booking or from scratch.

From a Booking

Go to the Inbox, locate your booking (filter on Linear TV/VOD or search by booking ID). Click on the booking, tick the checkbox, and press "Create CRI". Most fields auto-populate from the booking data.

Can't find a booking? Use the "Request Product Link" button in the Inbox.

From Scratch

Click "Create" from the nav bar. Media type defaults to Linear TV/VOD. Enter the client name, product name, campaign dates, and first transmission month. Then add clock numbers manually or from the copy library.

Essential Reference

The 30-hour broadcast clock

UK television uses a 30-hour clock. The broadcast day starts at 06:00 and runs to 29:59 (which is 05:59 the next calendar day). You need this for time bands.

Standard Times

21:45 → Band 21:00 – 21:59
22:41 → Band 22:00 – 22:59
19:15 → Band 19:00 – 19:59

After Midnight

00:30 → 24:30 → Band 24:00 – 24:59
01:30 → 25:30 → Band 25:00 – 25:59
05:00 → 29:00 → Band 29:00 – 29:59

CARIA time band fields default to 06:00 – 29:59 (whole broadcast day). For cherry pick campaigns, always narrow to the specific hour or 30-minute window.
05
Rotation Types

How creatives rotate

R

Ratio

Multiple creatives share slots with a defined split. Add all clocks to one block, set ratio numbers (e.g. 1:1 for equal, 2:1 for weighted). Ratios must be whole numbers, max 10.

S

Sequence

Creatives play in a specific order, then repeat. Add clocks to one block, assign sequence positions (1, 2, 3…). Traffic plays them in that fixed order.

C

Cherry Pick

Specific creatives assigned to specific spots — no randomness. Requires individual detail blocks per unique date/station/time combination. The most complex type.

06
Advanced

Cherry Pick campaigns

The most labour-intensive rotation type. You'll receive a schedule with exact spot assignments and must create detail blocks for each one.

1. Break down the rotation data

Pull out each spot: date, station/sales house, time, creative name, and clock number.

2. Map to station groups

Match each spot to a CARIA station group: C4-NETWORK, Owned, Partners, or C4 Streaming. Check the schedule for which channel each spot airs on.

3. Plan detail blocks

Group spots by: date + station group + time band (hour). If two spots share the same group, merge into one block with both clocks at ratio 1:1.

4. Create blocks in CARIA

For each group: insert a new empty block, select stations, reveal date range and time band, add clocks, and save.

5. Check for clashes

CARIA flags red warnings if blocks overlap. Fix by merging duplicates or adjusting ranges.

6. Fix CRI dates & send

Verify the "To" date hasn't auto-narrowed in Step 1. Then send to both traffic departments.

Cherry Pick — Pro Tip

30-minute window splitting

When two spots share the same hour but need different creatives, split the hour into two 30-minute windows.

Problem

Two spots at 22:27 and 22:42 on the same date and station, each needing a different clock. A 1:1 ratio means traffic can assign either randomly.

Day-of-week splitting

When a campaign needs different time bands on different days (e.g. Sunday vs weekdays), use the "Days of the week" option in "Show more / less detail" to split.

Scenario

Station Groups

Channel 4 stations

When clicking "Select stations" in a detail block, you'll see these groups under the Channel 4 tree.

C4-NETWORK

Channel 4 main network. The flagship linear channel.

Owned

E4, E4 Extra, More4, Film4, 4Seven, and related channels.

Partners

Third-party partner channels carried by Channel 4 sales.

C4 Streaming

VOD / streaming inventory. Required for C4 Streaming Traffic Dept.

Important: To send to C4 Streaming Traffic Dept, you must select both "Owned" AND "C4 Streaming" on your detail blocks. Without "C4 Streaming" selected, the traffic department won't see any updates and its checkbox will be greyed out.

Watch Out

Known gotchas

Real issues discovered through hands-on use. These will trip you up if you don't know about them.

CRI dates auto-narrow

Every save may shrink the CRI date range to match the narrowest range across your detail blocks.

Fix: Always check Step 1 and reset the "To" date after detail block changes. Do this last before sending.

Session drops / timeout

CARIA sessions can drop without warning — especially if left idle. You'll get a blank page or login screen.

Fix: Navigate back to cri.peachmedia.com. Your credentials usually auto-fill. Re-open the CRI from the Outbox — saved work is preserved.

Delete triggers confirm dialog

Clicking Delete pops up a browser confirmation that automation tools can't dismiss.

Fix: Warn the user before deleting that they'll need to click OK on the popup manually.

Time field corruption

Typing into time band fields can produce corrupted values like "22::0" instead of "22:00".

Fix: Use form_input (set value) rather than typing. Always verify time values after setting.

Step 3 nav link crashes

Clicking "3. review / send" from within Step 2 can cause a 404 or session crash.

Fix: Save from Step 2 → Outbox → click row → "View / Send". Never click Step 3 directly.

C4 Streaming checkbox greyed out

If detail blocks only have "Owned" stations, C4 Streaming Traffic Dept won't detect changes.

Fix: Add both "Owned" AND "C4 Streaming" station groups to enable sending to both traffic depts.
